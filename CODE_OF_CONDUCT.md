# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in the Kindario project respectful, inclusive, and constructive for everyone.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Writing issues, pull requests, commits, and documentation in clear English.
- Giving feedback that is direct, specific, and respectful.
- Staying focused on the quality, safety, accessibility, and integrity of the project.
- Respecting different perspectives, lived experiences, and professional backgrounds.
- Sharing criticism of code or decisions without attacking people.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Personal attacks, insults, or sustained hostility.
- Publishing private or sensitive information without permission.
- Spam, deliberate disruption, or low-quality noise in project discussions.
- Misleading claims about donations, humanitarian outcomes, or nonprofit impact.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers may edit, remove, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, commits, or other content that violates this Code of Conduct.

## Reporting

Report concerns privately through the repository owner on GitHub or through the contact route described in the [Security Policy](SECURITY.md).

All reports will be reviewed in good faith and handled as fairly as possible.
